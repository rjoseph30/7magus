User Interface and Flow

Lesson Plan Maker V1

Our collaboration on the development of Lesson Plan Maker marked a significant milestone in our journey through Structured Programming 1. This app, is both my design concepts and Sean Wilton Rodriguez's programming expertise, represents the culmination of our efforts in the subject.

Designed with the user experience in mind, Lesson Plan Maker boasts an intuitive interface that prioritizes simplicity and accessibility. Its primary aim is to streamline the lesson planning process for educators, particularly those within the academic context of Technological University of the Philippines Cavite.

Through the app, users are empowered to effortlessly input the necessary details for crafting lesson plans. By following a user-friendly format, educators can efficiently generate semi-detailed plans that are not only comprehensive but also catered to the needs of students within the institution.

Lesson Plan Maker V2

This final project for our Structured Programming 1 course, the Lesson Plan Maker, is a useful tool created to simplify the process of crafting lesson plans. Users can easily generate a ready-to-use template tailored for our university by filling in the input boxes provided by the app.

Designed and developed by Sean Wilton Rodriguez and me, Robert Joseph Eusebio, we are now working to enhance it further for our Structured Programming 2 course with Dazle Salvedia and Julieanne Castro.

This app aims to assist both pre-service and current teachers by making their work easier, even without internet access, as the app is available offline. It seeks to transform traditional teaching methods into a more efficient process.

Poster and Creative Designs

Social Awareness Poster:
Animal Cruelty

A social awareness poster addressing animal cruelty in our country highlights the plight of neglected stray animals and those subjected to abuse for entertainment or without justification. The message underscores the importance of treating animals with compassion and equality.

The poster features poignant images of suffering animals, symbolizing their lack of voice. As responsible members of society, we are urged to take action and advocate for these vulnerable animals, giving them the support they deserve.

As members of society, we have a moral responsibility to intervene and advocate for these vulnerable animals. This involves not only raising awareness but also actively supporting organizations and initiatives dedicated to animal welfare. By fostering a culture of empathy and education, we can encourage responsible pet ownership, promote adoption over buying pets, and advocate for stronger laws against animal cruelty.

This poster was created using Canva as part of a weekly project for our GEC6 Arts Appreciation course.

Movie Poster:
All The Bright Places

A newly designed movie poster features "All the Bright Places," directed by Brett Haley and starring Justice Smith and Elle Fanning. The film revolves around two troubled teenagers facing different challenges. They meet on the ledge of their school's bell tower, where Finch intervenes as Violet contemplates jumping off the bridge. As their friendship grows, Finch encourages Violet to step beyond her comfort zone, while Violet supports Finch in confronting the voices in his mind. Together, they embark on a journey of self-discovery and healing, exploring both the beauty and pain of life.

The movie delves into themes of mental health, grief, loss, and the profound impact of human connection. It portrays the intricate emotions of adolescence and how trauma shapes young lives. "All the Bright Places" is celebrated for its sensitive depiction of mental illness and has received acclaim for the performances of its lead actors and its heartfelt narrative.

This poster was created using Canva as part of a weekly project for our GEC6 Arts Appreciation course.

Blog post: Videogame

Back during my senior high school days, I created a blog post discussing the positive impact of video games on our well-being. While the prevailing notion often associates video games with addiction and negative effects on mental health, I argued that they can actually be beneficial. In my opinion, video games serve as a means to alleviate stress, providing a temporary escape from life's challenges. Additionally, I highlighted how gaming can enhance critical thinking and strategic skills, offering a space for relaxation and enjoyment.

Magazine: Dionysus

For our Arts Appreciation class, I undertook a magazine design project with a contemporary twist inspired by Greek mythology. This assignment was a recurring weekly task, and I'm proud to mention that my work secured the top spot in the Male category.

My design drew inspiration from Dionysus, the Greek god associated with wine and pleasure. Like the rest of the Twelve Olympians, Dionysus possessed immortality and formidable powers. Notably, he had the ability to produce wine and stimulate the growth of vines. Additionally, he could shapeshift into various animals such as bulls or lions. Among his unique abilities was the capacity to drive mortals into states of madness.
